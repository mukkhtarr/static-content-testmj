As a Cannabis business owner, it’s important to have a general understanding of the laws and best practices related to your role as an employer. The resources on this page outline some areas you should be aware of when it comes to worker’s rights.

Understanding labor peace agreements

  • Labor peace agreements for cannabis businesses

    State law requires many commercial cannabis licensees to enter into, and abide by, labor peace agreements. Recent changes to state law have strengthened these requirements, including by expanding the number of licensees that are subject to these requirements. Workplace safety

  • Labor violations or wage theft

    Experiencing labor violations or wage theft?

    File a complaint with the Department of Industrial Relations.

  • Cannabis industry health and safety

    CAL/OSHA (The Division of Occupational Safety and Health) provides detailed information about what is expected from employers when it comes to the health and safety of their employees.

    Requirements include:

    • Establishing an Injury and Illness Prevention Program
    • Following the occupational health and safety regulations that may apply to cannabis businesses
    • Enrolling in the CAL/OSHA 30-hour Standards for General Industry Training (not the federal OSHA 30-hour training).

    For help and resources, visit the Department of Industrial Relations website.
