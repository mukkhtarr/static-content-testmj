Executive Summary

Business and Professions Code section 26190 established annual reporting requirements for the Department of Cannabis Control (Department). Beginning March 1, 2023, and annually thereafter, the Department must prepare and submit to the Legislature an annual report on activities within the Department for the prior fiscal year. The report also provides updated data about the Equity Fee Waiver and Deferral Program for the prior fiscal year. Finally, the enclosed report includes the Department’s responses pursuant to the Budget Act of 2022, under the Business, Consumer Services and Housing Item 1115-102-0001, which requires, beginning in 2023, and until 2026, the Department to provide a report on the Cannabis Local Jurisdiction Retail Access Grant Program.

As required by section 26190 of the Business and Professions Code and Item 1115-102-0001 of the Budget Act of 2022, the Department submits the following information regarding its activities. This report contains the required information and data for the prior fiscal year, July 1, 2022 to June 30, 2023.

Processing time

The average time for processing state license applications by state license category.

  • Cultivation Licenses: 258 days
  • Manufacturing Licenses: 190 days
  • Distribution Licenses: 215 days
  • Testing Laboratory Licenses: 964 days
  • Retailer Licenses: 162 days
  • Microbusiness Licenses: 198 days
  • Event Organizer Licenses: 96 days
  • Temporary Cannabis Event Licenses: 107 days

The average times provided above are impacted by a number of external and internal factors, including, but not limited to: delays in responding to Department inquiries by applicants missing necessary documentation to meet annual license application requirements, verifying the accuracy of records submitted by applicants, and correspondence with other local and state entities to ensure compliance of statutory and regulatory requirements. Average licensing processing times are also impacted because many applicants do not produce all required documentation in a timely manner, creating significant delays in the application process. These factors, as well as a host of other factors, can impact the time it takes to approve a license. The Department notes that the processing of a backlog of over 6,000 applications from the previous reporting period (July 12, 2021 – June 30, 2022) also impacted processing times during this reporting period.

Testing laboratory applications are a notable outlier given the complexity of the license review process due to the requirement for a testing laboratory to submit complete standard operating procedures and method validations that meet regulatory testing standards. *Please see Exhibit 2 for a breakdown by state license category.

Enforcement activities

The number and type of enforcement activities conducted by the department and by local law enforcement agencies in conjunction with the department.

  • Since the Department consolidated in July 2021, our Enforcement Division has seized over $1.6 billion worth of product from the illegal marketplace and has significantly expanded law enforcement actions, with an over 98 percent increase from the previous fiscal year.
  • The Enforcement Division also led and assisted in the service of 386 search warrants. As a result of these actions, a total of 358,335 pounds of untested cannabis product was seized, which included the eradication of 658,244 plants, worth an estimated retail value of $593,283,003. In addition, these search warrant operations resulted in the seizure of 152 firearms (up 57 percent from the prior fiscal year), 107 arrests, and the seizure of $909,867 in cash.
  • Of these, the Department was the lead agency in 222 of these operations. In the Department-led operations, a total of 204,585 pounds of cannabis was seized, 360,468 plants were eradicated, 67 firearms were seized, and 26 arrests were made. The total estimated retail value of the seized product and plants was $338,651,553. Of the remaining 164 operations in which the Department assisted, a total of 153,751 pounds of cannabis was seized, 297,776 plants were eradicated, 85 firearms were seized, and 81 arrests were made. The total estimated retail value of the seized product and plants was $254,631,450.
  • The Compliance Division assisted in the service of 118 search warrants to support local or state law enforcement address unlicensed cultivation activity. These actions resulted in 22,531 pounds of cannabis product seized and 156,842 plants seized. The total estimated value of the seized product and plants was $166,570,800.
