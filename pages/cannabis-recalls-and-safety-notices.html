The cannabis recall process plays an important role in ensuring defective or potentially unsafe products are removed from the commercial market. There are two types of recalls: mandatory recalls, which are ordered by the Department, and voluntary recalls, which are conducted by the cannabis business.

About the recall process

Mandatory recalls

  • Ordered by DCC
  • Occurs when there is an immediate and serious threat to human life or health, and other remedies would cause an unreasonable delay in recalling the product from the market  
  • Licensee(s) are required to immediately cease distribution and recall the cannabis or cannabis product from the public or other licensees. 
  • DCC notifies all licensees who have the affected product and provides guidance about next steps. 

Voluntary recalls

  • Conducted by the licensee  
  • Occurs when the licensee identifies that their product is defective or unsafe (adulterated or misbranded). 
  • A licensee must notify DCC that they are initiating a recall.  
  • DCC will then work with licensees to ensure appropriate notices are provided and the recalled cannabis and cannabis products are properly handled. 
  • DCC posts the information to the list of voluntary recalls on its website, and sends a consumer advisory to provide information to licensees and consumers about the recall.
